The Scott Adams Strategy

Scott Adams, the author of Dilbert, outlined a simple one-pager on general financial advice. The advice is straightforward, but let's break it down a little.

Make A Will

Name beneficiaries on all of your investment vehicles and assets.

Pay Off Your Credit Card

Credit card balances usually carry double-digit interest rates. Since the balances have the highest interest rates of all debt that you're likely to accumulate, it's best to pay these off first.

Get Term Life Insurance

If you have a family to support, purchase term life insurance. Although term life insurance has an end date, it's much cheaper and it's much easier to understand than permanent insurance.


Max Out Your 401(k)

The 2019 contribution limit is $28,500. Some company plans with a Roth 401(k) option may enable you to save beyond this limit, allowing you to contribute an additional $27,500 of after-tax money.

Max Out Your IRA

The 2019 contribution limit is $6,500. Depending on your tax circumstances, you can elect to save this money in a Traditional IRA or a ROTH IRA.

Buy A House

If you can afford a house, buy one and live in it. Real estate is the largest investment that most Americans will make.


Save 6 Months Of Expenses

Put this money in a high-yield savings account. Not only will this sum of money accumulate interest over time, it will also be available to you in case of an emergency.

Invest The Remainder

Open an account at a discount brokerage and invest 70% of your remaining money in a low-cost stock index fund, and the other 30% in a low-cost bond fund. Do not touch this until retirement.

Hire A Fiduciary

If you have any extenuating circumstances, e.g. college planning, a tax audit, or retirement, hire a fee-based fiduciary, not someone who will charge a percentage of your investment portfolio.
